I worked with the digital marketing team to design and implement a landing page for lead generation.

Then, I redesigned the page to increase our conversion rate and improve the flow of the old design.

Phase 1. Initial Solution

Low-Fidelity Wireframe

I worked with the digital marketing team to design a lead generation wireframe and initial mockup for our landing page.

We completed the low fidelity wireframe using UI/UX best practices for an ecommerce lead generation page.

Together, we made sure to emphasize the importance of having the correct value propositions, form fills, and captivating copy in our design.

Wordpress Implementation

Video of old design

After we finished the mockup, I implemented the design into our live Wordpress site, including all the necessary tracking pixels used for google analytics, hotjar, and Hubspot.

However, after a few slow weeks of low quality leads, we realized we weren't getting the conversion rates we wanted. We wanted leads that were higher quality and more suited for the kind of intricate work our team was looking for.

Phase 2. Problem with the Initial Design

Goal: I studied recordings of our users on our site and our competitors to better understand how to improve our conversion rate.

User Research

Using Hotjar, I studied recordings and heatmaps of the most common areas of interest on the landing page.

Takeaways:

- Users kept clicking icons that weren't meant to be clicked.

- Users scrolled away from the hero section before reading it.

- Users scrolled to the bottom of the page without reading any of the value propositions.

- Users liked to click images, presumably hoping to navigate to another, more informational page.

Competitive Analysis

At this time, in order to improve the sales process of the digital marketing team, we researched other similar agencies in Orange County.

We studied the websites and contacted agencies to have a better understanding of their sales processes, targeted audience demographics, and leverage over other agencies.

Takeaways:

- Websites often included captivating graphics and moving imagery.

- Websites made it very clear, using just a few concise words, what their agency was all about. They usually kept this above the fold.

- Bold colors and a lot of pictures made it easier to flow through their site and to understand their services and mission.

Phase 3. Redesigning the Landing Page

Finally, I updated our design by aligning the visual elements with our style guide.

1. Brighter Colors

I changed the colors to make the overall page look brighter, and to match the rest of the site's style (light blue, with additional highlight colors).

2. Flow of website

I went back to the team to improve the copywriting for our value propositions. Including some market research on our competitors, we changed our selling points to better fit a niche in the market.

In order to make it easier for users to read each item, I added images and white space to spread each paragraph over a greater area.

Redesigned Implementation

3. Hero section

Changing the dark colored hero section into a video background because Hotjar recordings showed people quickly scrolling past the form section.

Hopefully the video would help attract the eyes to the moving background, which would mean they read and fill out the most important part of the page.

Redesigned Implementation

Conclusion

    By studying the recordings, I realized that users generally digest information better when they have images to guide and break up the flow of the website. By studying heatmaps, I understood how users expected certain elements to lead them to another page, while elements we actually wanted them to interact with were completely passed over.

    After updating the live site to reflect the changes, we immediately saw an increase in the number of conversions, and therefore, higher quality leads.

Reflection

    This project challenged me because I had to implement the whole design onto Wordpress. There were constraints I had not encountered before, especially regarding the Wordpress interface.

    However, I enjoyed working with live data. For example, getting feedback in the form of web recordings and heatmaps helped me decide what kind of changes I wanted to make to the existing design.

    In addition, I improved my teamwork and communication skills as I worked with the digital marketing team in order to make this website work.
